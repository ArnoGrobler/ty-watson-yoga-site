In seasonal theory, summer corresponds to the element of fire: visible, available and expressive. Summer asks us to move into the world of other people, what we want, what's working and what isn't. Our relationships, creativity and exhaustion are all turned up, and the lines between fact and fiction can become blurred.

Summer's quest is about the quality of the attention we bring to everything, not the quantity of effort, requiring our generosity and boundaries in equal measure. The seasonal series is about learning how to stay present without scattering, overextending, or burning out.

The series includes

  • Weekly practice and breath work, followed by the common room (Tuesdays 6 – 8pm Paris time)
  • A weekend theory workshop — giving us the wider map of summer's myths and metaphors, the "codes" of the season (date TBC)
  • One 60-minute online private session, scheduled during the month
  • Reading, prompts, and correspondence throughout
  • A short practice that repeats through the season — something you can take home and deepen, or keep for next summer

Common rooms are where we gather after practice to ask questions, swap ideas, and share what we're noticing. Come for a few minutes or stay the whole hour. Each week there's a prompt or question to give the conversation a shape, but there's nothing to prepare. You bring yourself and whatever you're up to.

The reading is an invitation, never homework. A book, an essay, a podcast, something where summer shows up clearly. Read all of it, some of it, or none of it — it's there to give us something to think about together.

The private session is yours to use: personal practice, clarifying the theory, or refining your own teaching.

This is a small, held group that gets to know each other over the month.

Each season stands alone and you don't need the previous ones to join, but returning students will find threads that carry through. It's for practitioners, teachers, and anyone curious about developing a more literate relationship with the seasons.

Ty is a yoga teacher based in Paris. He runs a Seasonal Series that explores the year through yoga, reading and conversation — part of a larger move towards a slower, more intentional way of teaching and living. Ty writes on his Substack, Small Adjustments, where he includes personal observations, teaching updates, and the occasional reading recommendation or playlist.

His teaching brings clear thinking and grounded, embodied practice into the same room. He wants poses to be coupled with something to keep your mind present and your imagination available. In class, expect precise physical practice alongside plenty of room to think. Ty appreciates techniques that are simple and repeatable, and he considers cultivating a practice and teaching part of living an artful life.

His perspective includes Hatha yoga, Daoism, philosophy, and the Katonah Yoga® canon. He believes that a teacher's job is not to fix anyone but to help them reorganize and reorient. Ty leans on metaphor and cultural references in his teaching, and is inspired by how words let us communicate, reconcile dilemmas, and tell our stories.

He teaches weekly online classes, leads immersions and trainings, and works privately with students to build a practice that supports their wellbeing and self-reflection. He also offers consulting and mentorship to students and fellow teachers. Over the last decade he has taught throughout the US, Canada and Europe. He is a certified Katonah Yoga® teacher, an E-RYT 200, and a Yoga Alliance Continuing Education Provider.

Born and raised in South Africa, he lived in New York City for several years before moving to Paris with his husband and their dog. He welcomes inquiries for private sessions, collaborations, and retreats.
